What is Ocean Conservation?

Key Takeaways

  • Oceans cover over 70% of the Earth's surface and are vital for climate regulation.
  • Overfishing threatens marine biodiversity and disrupts ocean ecosystems.
  • Plastic pollution is one of the biggest threats to marine life, harming fish, birds, and other sea creatures.
  • Coral reefs, which support a quarter of marine species, are dying due to rising ocean temperatures and acidification.
  • Reducing single-use plastics and properly disposing of waste helps protect marine life.
  • Oil spills and chemical pollution poison ocean habitats and impact marine food chains.
  • Marine protected areas (MPAs) help preserve biodiversity and restore fish populations.
  • Climate change is causing sea levels to rise and increasing the frequency of extreme weather events.
  • Sustainable seafood choices support healthier fish populations and prevent overfishing.
  • Individual actions, like reducing carbon footprints and supporting conservation organizations, contribute to ocean protection.
