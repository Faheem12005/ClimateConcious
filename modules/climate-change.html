What is Climate Change?

Key Takeaways

  • Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns.
  • Human activities, especially burning fossil fuels, are the primary cause of climate change.
  • Greenhouse gases like CO₂ trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming.
  • Rising global temperatures are causing more frequent and severe weather events.
  • Melting ice caps and glaciers contribute to rising sea levels, threatening coastal communities.
  • Deforestation reduces the planet’s ability to absorb CO₂, worsening climate change.
  • Oceans are becoming more acidic due to increased carbon dioxide absorption.
  • Shifting climate patterns disrupt agriculture, food supply, and biodiversity.
  • Transitioning to renewable energy and sustainable practices can slow climate change.
  • Individual actions, such as reducing waste and conserving energy, help combat climate change.
