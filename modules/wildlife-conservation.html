What is Wildlife Conservation?

Key Takeaways

  • Wildlife conservation protects animal species and their natural habitats.
  • Biodiversity is essential for ecosystem stability and human survival.
  • Deforestation and habitat destruction are major threats to wildlife.
  • Illegal poaching and wildlife trafficking endanger many species.
  • Climate change affects animal migration patterns and food availability.
  • Protected areas, such as national parks and wildlife reserves, help safeguard species.
  • Pollution, including plastic waste, harms marine and terrestrial wildlife.
  • Conservation efforts include breeding programs and habitat restoration projects.
  • Reducing human-wildlife conflict helps protect both animals and communities.
  • Individuals can support conservation by reducing waste, avoiding wildlife products, and supporting sustainable practices.
